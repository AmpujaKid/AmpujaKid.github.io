Spain gave America its Philippine colony as a part of the terms in the Treaty of Paris when it lost the Spanish-American War of 1898.

---

Two days before the U.S. Senate ratified the treaty, the Filipino nationalists fought back against the American Forces. They were led by Emilio Aguinaldo, who wished for the Philippines to be independent rather than a change in colonial rulers.

---

The Philippine-American War that followed lasted three years (1899-1902).

Casualties American: 4,200
Filipino: 220,000
